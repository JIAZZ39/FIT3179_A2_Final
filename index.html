DEMOGRAPHIC TRENDS AND PUBLIC HEALTH
IN MALAYSIA

Tracing population changes and their impact on national well-being

Birth Rate Across Malaysia (2021)

Mapping regional variations in new life.

Population Pyramid (2021)

The largest cohorts are males and females aged 10-29, indicating a large youth population that will be entering the workforce and starting families over the next decade.

This demographic momentum suggests continued population growth in the near future.

There is a noticeable surplus of males compared to females, particularly in the 0-9 and 20-39 age groups.

Death Rate Across Malaysia (2021)

Highlighting regional disparities in mortality.

Communicable Disease Trends by Category (2011-2021)

Tracking a decade of infectious disease patterns and shifts.

Air Pollutant Concentration Trend (2017-2021)

The positive trend of improving air quality suggests reduced industrial and transport activity. Notably, the 2021 composition is still dominated by CO and particular matter, highlighting them as key areas for continued focus.

Natural Population Growth in Malaysia (2017-2021)

The slowing natural population growth suggests a societal shift towards smaller family sizes, possibly linked to ongoing urbanization, higher education levels, and increased costs of living.

The particularly sharp increase of deaths in 2021 strongly suggests an additional, significant impact from excess mortality during the COVID-19 pandemic.
